Aadhaar Tech

I was required to design the visual identity and digital interfaces of the new product division of Labwire Tech Pvt Ltd called Aadhaar Tech, which focuses on providing Aadhaar based technological solutions.

Worked in the capacity of a freelance visual designer.

April 2017 - May 2017

ROLE

  • - Development of the visual identity system. Includes logomarks and other brand elements.
  • - Requirement analysis through stakeholder feedbacks. Information obtained through interviews and questionnaires.
  • - Conceptualizing and designing the user interface for the initial prototype. Expected to be implemented for the Beta lauch.

GOAL

The new division, Aadhaar Tech, aims at bringing accessible Aadhaar-based API information calls to users. Aadhaar is the world’s largest biometric ID system and is currently used in India. It is a unique 12-digit number that can be obtained by the residents of India, using their biometric and demographic information. Aadhaar numbers are linked with a host of services, such as banking accounts, mobile sim cards, e-signatures and several welfare schemes. UIDAI is a statutory authority established in January 2009 by the government of India that oversees Aadhaar. Building on the API’s provided by UIDAI, Aadhaar Tech’s API Dashboard provides a seamless interaction between developers and their data.

The logo of Aadhaar, the world’s largest biometric system.

The stakeholders insisted on having a visual identity that would make the brand stand out from the ocean of developer targeted technology. The initial vision of the product was to exhibit a fresh yet functional appeal amongst developers and amateur coding enthusiasts alike. The dashboard had to also cater to users who might not be developers but rather just need to implement Aadhaar based services.

VISION

The visual identity of the product focuses on its primary service, Aadhaar. Biometrics are an integral part of Aadhaar schemes and thus are incorporated into the products of Aadhaar Tech as well. The fingerprint motif forms the crux of the brand along with vibrant yet calm colour tones. It also helps bring in a human element to a visual space that is predominantly occupied with barebone elements that developers have been acclimatised to.
