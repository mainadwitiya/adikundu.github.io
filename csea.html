CSE Association

I worked on the development of the visual identity of CSE Association, the student-run association of the Department of Computer Science and Engineering, SRM IST.

Worked on the design and media of the events organized by CSEA in the academic year 2017-2018

August 2017 - March 2018

ROLE

  • - Development of the revised logo and a visual identity system.
  • - Designed additional brand elements such as ID cards, lanyards and social media deliverables.
  • - Led a team of 80+ undergraduate students and organized 4 events such as fests and competitions in our campus.

GOAL

The task at hand was the development of a fresh, unique brand experience for CSEA that would establish its visual identity within the campus and improve the reach of the print and social media. 

A new identity would also help it stand out from other departmental associations and clubs that exist within the institute. 

Most of these departmental associations’ logos lack a certain sense of originality. They conform to a standard template followed by amateurly executed ‘tech’ logos. Furthermore, the variety of visual media produced by the organisations were not coherent with the logos and their respective themes.

Another notable insight was that these logos often tend to highlight a certain element of the department’s study, for example, microprocessor circuits.

VISION

But what about Computer Science?

After initial interviews of my fellow CSE undergrads, their message became clear stating that Computer Science, as a field, is too expansive to be represented by one visual motif. Hence, a pattern library was created to be the essence of the identity.

Each core pattern of this identity represents a certain part of Computer Science; which is present in our curriculum and taught on a daily basis.

This dynamic pattern library also serves as the reminder of continuous change in the organisation. As seniors graduate and freshermen join CSEA each year, the core team of the organisation is created anew annually.

This is also parallel to the dynamism of CS as an academic field. Core curriculum subjects such as Data Structures and Algorithms that are being taught now, will be accompanied or possibly even subsumed by electives such as Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence in the upcoming years. The core patterns of the brand system can be supplanted by those designed by the newer generation of students which represent their fields of interest.

The following results were observed after the implementation of the new visual identity:

- A growth of 40% on the likes of the Facebook page of CSEA over the academic year 2017-18.

- This academic year’s events have received an approximate total of 840 participants across 4 different competitions and fests.

- The revamped branding allowed for an increase in sponsorship tie-ups with companies such as McDonald’s, Red Bull and HackerRank; drastically improving over organizations in the previous year.

- The ID card lanyard design proved to be massively popular amongst students and was successful in creating the ‘marketing hype’ that it was designed for.

In conclusion, through systemic design strategies, the Association was able to form a strong emotional connection with its participants and other students in the campus.

The lanyard was the most iconic element of the brand.

The branding cemented the image of a revamped Association.
